Thinking and Mindsets

A Little About Me and My Mind

12/04/2014

So coming into Dev Bootcamp, we had to take a test to figure out our individual learning styles. My test came back as Abstract Sequential (you can learn more here) The gist of this style is it allows me to visualize, to conceive ideas, to understand or believe that which I cannot actually see in a linear and logical fashion. Ok so that sounds pretty boring upfront. At first, my thought was "Oh great, some label I have to stick to now." But after some digging, I realize there is much more to this. I found that this isn't something designed to box me in. Rather, it is there to provide direction. To show me where I am, and where to go from there.

One of the characteristics of my learning type is that we prefer to learn/work alone. Part of the DBC cirriculum is to pair program regularly. As you can see, these two things do not mix. Now that I am aware of what is making me shy away from pairing, I can find ways to overcome that obstacle. Another draw back that comes with my learning style is an aversion to working with people of differing viewpoints. I can already tell that I will encounter many new perspectives during the course of this camp. I am already mentally prepping myself to be open minded and aware of where people are coming from.

Luckily it isn't all struggles and things to overcome. Two of the ways that I learn best is with access to experts and references when needed and by being in a fast paced and stimulating environment. DBC is going to be the perfect place for these things. I know that the teachers are going to be fantastic aids to my learning. Part of the reason I applied was because I knew I needed these teachers. I also knew that the fast pace is something that I am going to thrive in.

As I go deeper into DBC, I plan to put myself in all the situations that are normally uncomfortable or different for me. I want to interact with people who think differently. I want to work with people more. I think that I will be able to broaden my thinking so much more the further into this we go.

Until next time,
Zac
